# Procurement
- The process of acquiring goods, services or works from *external sources* in a strategic manner.
- Strategic *end-to-end process* of *sourcing, negotiating and acquiring* goods, services or works from external suppliers to *meet organizational needs.* 
- Procurement Includes;
	- Spending Money
	- Acquiring resources
	- Supporting Operations
# IT Procurement
- Process of identifying, sourcing, acquiring and managing *technology products and services* (**Hardware, Software, Cloud Infrastructure**) to meet business goals.
---
## Key Activities
### 1. Identifying Needs 
### 2. Selecting Suppliers
- Long term Suppliers
- Changing suppliers according to requirements
	Contracts(Limitations / Expiration)
	Budget
	Quality Deprecation / Better quality alternatives
### 3. Negotiating Constructs
### 4. Purchasing
### 5. Managing Supplier Relationships
---
## Traditional Procurement vs IT Procurement

| **Traditional Procurement** | **IT Procurement**                                            |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Physical Goods              | Hardware, Software, Cloud services                            |
| Short Lifecycle             | Rapid Technical Change                                        |
| Standard Suppliers          | Specialized vendors <br>ex: Ciso for Routers and DELL for PCs |
| Low Technical Risk          | High Technical Dependancy                                     |
