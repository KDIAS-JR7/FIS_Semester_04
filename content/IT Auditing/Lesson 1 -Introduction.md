# What is Auditing?
- The on-site verification activity, such as inspection or examination, of a process or quality system, to ensure compliance to requirements.
# What is IT Auditing?
- The systematic examination and evaluation of an organization’s information technology infrastructure, policies, and operations.
# Three types of Audits
## 1. External(Financial) Audits
- An *external, independent* expert checks and verifies the accuracy and fairness of a company's financial records and statements.
---
### Auditor(External Expert)
- The one expressing an opinion on the presentation of financial statements.
- In External audits, the auditor is an external entity, independent of the organization being audited. They do not have any affiliation with the organization and their assessment is hence better accepted by the public.
--- 
- It's known as the *attest service.*
- Performed by Certified Public Accountants(CPA) working for public accounting firms independent of the organization being audited.
### **Objective** 
	Assure fair presentation of financial records.
### **Key Concept** 
	Process of independence
- Public confidence on a organizations' internally produced financial records/statements depends on the evaluation of the external auditor.
### **Components** 
	- Auditing standards
	- A systematic process
	- Management Assertions and Audit Objectives
	- Obtaining Evidence
	- Ascertaining Materiality
	- Communicating Results
## 2. Internal Audits
- A independent appraisal function established within an organization to examine and evaluate its activities as a service to the organization.
### Activities
- Conduct Financial Audits
- Examine an operations' compliance with organizational policies
- Review organizations' compliance with legal obligations
- Evaluate operational efficiency
- Detecting and pursuing fraud within the firm
## 3. Fraud Audits
- The objective - to investigate anomalies and gather evidence of fraud that may lead to criminal conviction
---
## Internal Audits vs External Audits


|                  | **Internal**                                                                                                             | **External**                                                                      |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Frequency**    | Audit plan is determined annually, but audits happen on an area every 2-5 years                                          | Performed annually                                                                |
| **objective**    | Provide management with feedback on functioning of internal controls and areas with room for improvement and added value | Evaluate organizations' compliance with guidelines and regulations(HIPAA,GAAP...) |
| **Who?**         | Board of directors or management determine internal audit plan                                                           | External auditor                                                                  |
| **Reporting**    | Independent of the area being audited                                                                                    | Report to public                                                                  |
| **Users**        | Management of the subject being audited                                                                                  | Stakeholders external to the organization. Ex: Investors, Lenders, Customers      |
| **Requirements** | Have an internal audit function is preferable.                                                                           | Depends on the organizations' scale, external stakeholder requirements            |
| **Skills**       | Certifications on auditing are not required. Certifications on the field being audited is preferable                     | Certified Public Accountants(CPA) firms required                                  |

# Audit Risk
- Probability of an auditor rendering an unqualified opinion on a financial statement, that are in fact materially misstated.
- Material misstatements can be,
	Errors
	Irregularities
	Or both.
## Audit risk components
- Audit risk is based on the 'Ex-ante' values of the audit risk model
### 1. Inherent Risk
Inherent risk is generally a risk of financial statement errors or omissions that have taken place outside of the organisation’s internal controls. This type of risk is prevalent in highly complex, data-intensive and challenging transactions and financial accounts.
### 2. Control Risk
Unlike inherent risk, which takes place outside of internal controls, control risk occurs due to the failure of these internal controls. This could be, for example, errors creeping in during the preparation of a company’s financial statements, which would then affect the overall outcome of the audit.
### 3. Detection Risk
 Detection risk is straightforward – it is the risk that an auditor does not find mistakes in financial statements. Detection risk is defined by the ISA 200 as “The risk that the procedures performed by the auditor to reduce audit risk to an acceptably low level will not detect a misstatement that exists and that could be material, either individually or when aggregated with other misstatements.”

> **Audit Risk (AR) = Inherent Risk (IR) x Control Risk (CR) x Detection Risk (DR)**
