# WWW (World Wide Web)
 >The ==**World Wide Web (WWW)**== is an information system where documents and web resources are identified by URLs and linked by hyperlinks. Invented by `Tim Berners-Lee` at `CERN` in 1989, it is an application built _on top_ of the internet, allowing users to navigate public web pages.

# World Wide Web (WWW) Vs Internet

It’s easy to mix up the Web and the Internet, but they’re different:

| **World Wide Web**                                               | **Internet**                                                                            |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A collection of webpages and websites you access with a browser. | A global network connecting computers.                                                  |
| 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN.                                 | 1960s as [ARPANET.](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/computer-networks/arpanet-full-form/) |
| To share and explore information like text, images, and videos.  | To connect devices and share data.                                                      |
| Through browsers like Chrome or Firefox.                         | Through any connected device for email, apps, etc.                                      |
| Visiting a website like Wikipedia.                               | Sending an email or streaming a video.                                                  |
## Advantages and Disadvantages of WWW
## Advantages of the WWW
- **Global Access to Information:** Provides instant access to a vast, decentralized repository of educational resources, news, and research from around the world
- **Immediate Communication:** Connects people globally through Email, video conferencing, and social networks, bridging geographical gaps.
- **Economic & Business Opportunities:** Powers the massive growth of e-commerce, allowing businesses to operate globally at lower marginal costs and enabling consumers to shop and bank from anywhere.
- **Remote Work & Telemedicine:** Supports work-from-home capabilities and provides access to healthcare consultations remotely, increasing accessibility for remote or disabled users.
## Disadvantages of the WWW
- **Cybersecurity & Privacy Risks:** The open nature of the web makes users vulnerable to malware, hacking, data breaches, and identity theft when conducting financial or personal transactions
- **Misinformation & "Fake News":** Anyone can publish content, which makes it easy for unverified information, scams, and deliberate misinformation to spread and manipulate public opinion.
- **Time Wasting & Addiction:** Over-reliance on the web can lead to internet addiction, social isolation, reduced productivity, and health issues due to a sedentary lifestyle.
- **Exposure to Inappropriate Content:** The lack of universal regulation means users—particularly children and vulnerable individuals—can easily stumble upon adult content, hate speech, or malicious cyberbullying.
